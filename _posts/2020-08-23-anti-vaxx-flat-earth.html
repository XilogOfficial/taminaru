Anti-vaxx, Flat Earth, etc.

My view on it

While I personally do not agree with those beliefs, they are still interesting to think about and study on.
Obviously for example, with the Flat Earth idea, unless you bring a new possible mathematical proof the Earth is not round, you cannot deny science. But with Anti-vaxx some of the points are actually valid.

Examples:

  • Pharmacies do very much rip you off. Be it with marketing tactics or by just making the products very expensive.
  • Natural medicine is actually great for your body, provided you do not rely on just essential oils a lot of the functional natural alternatives to medicine may be faster, more effective and less expensive.

One particularly interesting detail about the people that support these ideologies is how it can apply to a very broad list of mentalities. And how a lot of the time these people show a very intense passion for protecting their point at a costs.


With that out of the way now, let's analyze how I think a person can start profoundly believing said topics:

  • Scenario 1

    The person is clueless and this is what made most sense to them.

  • Scenario 2 (Something I can agree to as well at some extent.)

    The person is aware of how many secrets are being kept to us (think: Anti-vaxx "Big Pharma" theory).

  • Scenario 3

    The person is a troll and just wants to annoy people.

    Let's face it, some do that, it is not new either.

  • Scenario 4

    The person does not care anymore, they are bored and just want to experience something new.

  • Scenario 5 --- The Band Wagon Effect

    They see the popularity of these theories and think they are true because of the number of followers.

There are obviously more possible scenarios but this is all that I can think of.
